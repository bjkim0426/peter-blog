Overview

The 2Good2Go Bot automates the process of reserving food deals through the TooGoodToGo app. Using Python, Appium, and Selenium WebDriver, the script interacts with the app's user interface to quickly identify when items become available, automatically attempts to reserve them, and handles the checkout process. The goal is to reduce the time it takes to secure high-demand items, increasing the chances of successfully completing a reservation.

Explanation of the Code

main.py

  • WebDriver Setup: The script uses Appium to connect to an iOS device through the specified device UDID. It then initializes the Appium driver with the appropriate capabilities to interact with the TooGoodToGo app.
  • Item Availability Check: The main function continuously checks for the availability of items by monitoring the screen for the "Reserve" button and pop-ups that indicate an item has sold out. Once an item is available, it attempts to click the "Reserve" button.
  • Popup Handling: If a pop-up appears indicating that the item is unavailable, the bot detects it and closes the pop-up before continuing the search.
  • Checkout Process: After successfully reserving an item, the bot proceeds through the checkout process, clicking "Got it" to acknowledge the prompt and automatically increasing the quantity of items. Finally, the bot triggers the "Buy with Apple Pay" button, leaving the final step for manual confirmation.

Script Mechanics

  • Item Reservation: The bot continuously checks for item availability, simulating clicks on the "Reserve" button once an item becomes available.
  • Popup Handling: If an item is not available and a "Sold Out" pop-up appears, the bot closes the pop-up and retries until an item is successfully reserved.
  • Checkout Process: The script automates the checkout by interacting with the interface, including increasing item quantities and triggering the payment process. The final confirmation requires manual user input.

Why I Developed the Bot

The TooGoodToGo app is a platform where businesses list unsold food items that are made that day before closing, aiming to reduce food waste. My sister-in-law was disappointed by the availability of a popular restaurant's offerings, which were often listed at a good value but sold out very quickly. She mentioned that she suspected others were using a bot to reserve the items, as they were consistently sold out within minutes of being listed.

Hearing this sparked my curiosity, and I decided to develop a customized bot specifically for her. After creating the bot, her success rate of reserving the items went from about 5% to an impressive 99.99%. It was exciting to see how automation could improve her chances and reserve the items she wanted, all while reducing the time and effort required to secure the best deals!
